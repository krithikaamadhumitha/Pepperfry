# Marketing Management – Pepperfry Case Study  

## Overview  
This project was developed as part of the **Executive General Management Program (EGMP) at IIM Trichy**.  
Pepperfry.com, founded in 2011, is India’s leading online furniture and home décor marketplace. With 150+ studios across 100+ cities and ~50% market share, Pepperfry has leveraged a managed marketplace model, robust logistics and customer‑centric strategies to become a dominant player in the e‑commerce furniture industry.  

## Objective  
To evaluate Pepperfry’s marketing management practices and customer experience strategy, applying academic frameworks to identify strengths, gaps and growth opportunities in the Indian furniture retail market.  

## Methodologies  
- **STP (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning):** Identified target segments and positioning strategy.  
- **Customer Decision Journey Mapping:** Tracked stages from need recognition → post‑purchase.  
- **Service Design Strategy:** Studied omnichannel integration, logistics, and customer support.  

## Key Insights  
- **First‑Mover Advantage:** Early entry secured ~50% market share.  
- **Omnichannel Presence:** Studio Pepperfry centers bridge online trust gaps.  
- **Tech Integration:** AR/VR tools enhance visualization and reduce purchase hesitation.  
- **Customer Advocacy:** Reviews, loyalty programs, and referral rewards drive repeat business.  
- **Competitive Edge:** Strong logistics and curated product range differentiate Pepperfry from rivals like Urban Ladder and IKEA.
